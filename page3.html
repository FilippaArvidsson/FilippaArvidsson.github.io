The Female Character Rig is a user-friendly and versatile rig. The controls are color-coordinated, with purple for controls related to the legs, red for arms, blue for the back and so on. The model was sculpted in ZBrush and then retopologized in Maya to allow for animation as well as optimization of the poly-count. A normal-map was also baked in ZBrush from the high-poly mesh to the low-poly, uv-mapped mesh.

The rig has also had its skin weights painted to work as intended when deformed. It utilizes IK-handles for arms and legs, with pole-vector controls for elbows and knees, as well as IK-splines for the back and hair. In this case, the fingers are controlled by forward kinematics rather than driven keys, to allow for more user control and a wider variety of poses. The reason for the legs and arms being controlled by inverse kinematics rather than forward, is that it is more straightforward to get the hand or foot to where you want it. Coupled with the pole-vector elbow and knee controllers, the animator still maintains a lot of control over the limb as a whole.

The purpose of this project is to show that I can:
- Sculpt characters in ZBrush
- Bake normal maps from a high-poly mesh in ZBrush
- Retopologize characters intended for animation in Maya
- Rig characters in Maya
- Use Inverse and Forward Kinematics
- Paint Skin Weights in Maya
